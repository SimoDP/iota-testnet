Some smaller things/suggestion from my side:
No need to use the word Rebased. The protocol is IOTA. Rebased was just one protocol upgrade.
Also no need to use rebased in the explorer urls. You can find all endpoint in our docs:
https://docs.iota.org/developer/network-overview
Also the explorer links didn't work for me 🤔 

Also didn't check your code as it is not formatted. If you didn't already it might make sense to share your git repo

Also for explanation I probably wouldn't combine gas and oracle server. I would consider them two different components in your architecture.
Also I'm wondering if there would be a better solution for the oracle. By using asymetric approach you could store a public key on chain and verify it. That would be your auth for the record_scan 🤔
Which would also actually be a good thing to fail tx on non auth tx. Because the attacker would loose money. 
For record_scan you btw don't need the gas station, right? As it isn't a tx send by the user. But by your infra.
But yeah your approach works.
Also you can't really revoke store caps (or other caps in that sense) in your system. Using VCs or Trust hierarchy where you have revokation option could theoretically help here.

Also you might want to think about a burn flow for end of lifetime? So no one can re-use that chip?

Last thing: In V2 instead of using a vector that links to your DID I would let the Luxury Asset directly own the DID. So every luxury asset ownd a DID which can have VCs linked to it. Basically a digital passport
